    Men’s Jeans for Every Fit and Style

    A great pair of jeans is the foundation of any wardrobe. Whether upgrading your go-to pair or trying something new, Gap men’s jeans offer comfort, durability, and everyday wearability. Designed to move with you and get better over time, our men’s denim collection has something for every occasion.

    If you prefer a tailored fit, our skinny jeans provide a modern, flexible feel that pairs well with a pocket tee, a Gap logo hoodie, and sneakers for an everyday uniform. For a relaxed vibe, men’s straight-leg jeans offer versatility—wear them with an Oxford shirt and loafers for a polished outfit or a cozy sweater when temperatures drop.

    Athletic builds will appreciate the athletic taper jeans, designed with extra room where it’s needed while keeping a structured fit. Try them with a Henley and leather boots for a put-together yet casual look. For vintage-inspired style, ‘90s jeans bring a relaxed shape and easy comfort. Pair them with a graphic t-shirt and an overshirt for a laid-back, throwback feel. If you prefer extra room, baggy jeans are the loosest fit in our lineup, great for weekend wear with a soft sweater and sneakers.

    The right wash can define any outfit. Men’s blue jeans are always a classic choice, while dark washes offer a more refined feel—think a blazer over a crisp button-down. For an edgier look, men’s black jeans pair well with a flannel or fitted polo. Light to medium washes and distressed finishes create a casual, worn-in look.

    For a fit that never goes out of style, men’s bootcut jeans are a reliable choice. Their subtle flare works well with everything from rugged work boots to casual sneakers. Pair them with a flannel shirt or a crewneck sweater for an easy, go-anywhere outfit.

    Whether you're heading to work, meeting up with friends, or keeping things low-key, Gap men’s jeans are designed for comfort, flexibility, and everyday wear. With a fit for every body type and a wash for every occasion, the right pair is waiting for you.
